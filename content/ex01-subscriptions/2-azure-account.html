Create an Azure Cloud Account

Follow these steps to create a personal Azure account for coursework.

Important: Your college-issued student EDU Azure Active Directory (AAD) email account is for Microsoft 365/Email access and may not support the Azure cloud services needed in this class. You will need to create a separate personal Azure cloud account to complete class exercises.

How do you pronounce "Azure?"

Most people have very strong opinions about this. There are two common pronunciations and both are acceptable.

Azure is from the french word azuré, and is very much a part of the English language. The preferred common pronunciation within the United States is AZH-uhr, while in the UK the emphasis is more commonly on the second syllable, az-ZHOOR.

But which do they use at Microsoft?

Having many friends and associates working at and for Microsoft and the Azure team, I can tell you first-hand that there really isn't a consensus. In Redmond, the American pronunciation is more common, and I have worked with Microsoft employees in Canada who unsurprisingly preferred the French pronunciation more common in the UK.

So, which should you use? Either is acceptable, but before you take a stand, neither is incorrect.

Before You Begin: What You Need

  • Personal Email Address (cannot be your student AAD / EDU login)
  • Phone Number (SMS verification)
  • Credit or Debit Card (identity verification)
  • Personal Information (name and address)

Step-by-Step Account Creation

1. Access the Azure Signup Page

Visit the Azure portal sign-up page:
https://azure.microsoft.com/en-us/free
Click Start free.

2. Sign in or Create a Microsoft Account

  • If you already have a personal Microsoft account (e.g., @outlook.com), sign in.
  • If not, click Create one! and set up a new Microsoft account using your personal email.

3. Enter Basic Profile Information

Provide your name, region, and identity details as requested.

4. Phone Verification

Enter your phone number and verify via SMS code.

5. Identity Verification (Credit/Debit Card)

Add a credit or debit card for verification. Microsoft may place a temporary authorization hold. No charges will occur unless you upgrade or exceed free limits.

6. Review Agreement and Finish

Agree to the terms and the Microsoft Azure Free Account conditions, then click Sign up.

7. Wait for Activation

Azure will provision your resources. This usually completes within minutes. Once done, you can access the portal at:
https://portal.azure.com

After Your Azure Account Is Active

Secure the Account

  • Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
  • Avoid using your root-level account for deployments (optional best practice)

Monitor Usage & Billing

  • Track consumption via the Cost Management + Billing dashboard
  • Review your $200 initial free credit (if offered)
  • Set Cost Alerts to avoid charges

Tips for Students

  • You may find offers for free Azure credits from Microsoft. This may change from time to time.
  • Phone verification is required before access is granted.
  • Azure free tier limits vary by service — monitor consumption.
